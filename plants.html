Orchid

A medicinal marvel cloaked in elegance. Orchids, especially Phalaenopsis, are safe, edible, and bursting with wellness potential.

🌿 Key Benefits:

  • 💆‍♀️ Skin: Rich in Vitamin A & antioxidants — reduces wrinkles, rejuvenates skin.
  • 🫃 Digestion: Relieves ulcers, acidity, constipation.
  • 👁️ Eyes: Packed with Vitamin C & E for better vision.
  • 🧠 Stress: Used traditionally for calming the mind.
  • 🛡️ Antimicrobial, Anti-inflammatory, Anti-cancer effects.
  • 🌿 Treats ailments from arthritis to snake bites (yes, for real).

🍽️ Edible Uses:

Orchid petals can be eaten raw in salads, stir-fried, or candied for desserts. In Zambia, the root is used to prepare Chikanda, a protein-rich dish often called "African polony".

Tulsi

A sacred herb in Ayurveda, Tulsi purifies the air and the body, and holds spiritual significance in Indian households.

🌿 Key Benefits:

  • Boosts immunity and fights infections.
  • Reduces stress and balances hormones.
  • Improves respiratory health.

🍽️ Traditional Uses:

Commonly used in kadhas, teas, and home remedies for colds, coughs, and flu. Often consumed as Tulsi tea.

Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera is a cooling, soothing plant with benefits for skin, digestion, and immunity.

🌿 Key Benefits:

  • Heals burns and hydrates skin.
  • Improves gut health and reduces inflammation.
  • Supports liver detoxification and boosts immunity.

🍽️ Edible Uses:

Aloe pulp is used in juices, smoothies, and herbal tonics. Often combined with honey or lemon.
